The immediate W I see:
1. You're Solving Negative Revenue (The Best Kind of Problem)
Most SaaS sells "make more money" (positive revenue - uncertain, hard to prove).
Promco sells "stop losing money you're already losing" (negative revenue - immediate, measurable).
Why this is a W:

Distributor can SEE the loss right now ("I ran promo, lost KES 150K")
Your tool prevents future loss (quantifiable immediately)
ROI is obvious: "I was losing 150K/month, now I'm not"
Negative revenue always converts better than positive revenue promises

Signal: When you show that profit warning on a REAL promo they're planning and they go "oh shit, I would've run that" - you've won. That's your immediate W.

2. FIFO Profit Tracking = Unfair Advantage
Everyone tracks:

❌ "Did they redeem the promo?" (vanity metric)
❌ "How much revenue?" (doesn't account for cost)
❌ "Stock levels" (but not which batch, what cost)

Only you track:

✅ Which SPECIFIC bottle was sold (oldest first)
✅ What it ACTUALLY cost (that batch's purchase price)
✅ Real profit on THAT redemption

Why this is a W:
This is data they literally don't have anywhere else. You're not competing with Excel or POS systems - you're creating a new category of visibility.
Signal: If distributors say "wait, you can tell me profit PER BOTTLE not just per SKU?" - you've differentiated.

3. Outcome-Based Pricing = Alignment
"Pay us KES 50K/month to use our tool" → resistance, negotiation, "let me think about it"
"Pay us 5% of profit we help you create" → if it works, you WANT to pay more.
Why this is a W:

Removes adoption risk (they only pay if it works)
Aligns incentives (you want them to be profitable)
Creates viral loop (the more successful they are, the more they promote you)

Signal: If they say "okay let's try it for one month" without negotiating price - you've removed friction.

You're not building "promo software."
You're building decision infrastructure.

Retailers deciding what to stock → inventory profit scoring

Why this is the biggest W:
You're not building a "liquor promo tool" - you're building profit intelligence for FMCG decisions. Liquor is just the wedge.
